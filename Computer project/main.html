Chapter 5: Consequences and Mentoring

Respect, Responsibility, and Growth — Rules Help Us Learn Better!

About This Regulation

The school has the authority to handle student-related cases in accordance with policies and considerations. This includes different levels of offenses: mild, moderate, and severe.

Mild Offenses

  • Handled by subject teachers or homeroom teachers (1st–4th offense)
  • Handled by Student Affairs starting from the 5th offense

Consequences (Ordered List)

  1. Verbal warning
  2. Red offense slip
  3. Community service
  4. Parent call and warning letters

Mentoring

Mentoring is done through the following:

  • Regular reporting to the Vice Principal of Student Affairs
  • Guidance from homeroom teachers
  • Counseling from the school counselor
